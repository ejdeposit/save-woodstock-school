SAVE OUR SCHOOL 救我们的学校

Last week, as part of the rollout of Phase 2 of the Southeast Guiding Coalition, PPS released draft proposals for enrollment, feeder patterns, and boundaries that drastically alter Woodstock Elementary School beginning in Fall 2022.

In one proposal, Woodstock would become a whole-school Mandarin program, and the students in the neighborhood program would be dispersed to neighboring elementary schools.

In the second proposal, the Mandarin program would move to Bridger Elementary, and Woodstock would become home to Creative Sciences School K-8. Boundaries will be redrawn and many neighborhood students will be zoned to different schools.

Both of these proposals divide our special community and create more disruption and uncertainty for our children.

Our community needs to stay engaged and make our voices heard. PPS is moving quickly with these proposals and plans to make a final decision on the fate of our school in January or February.  PLEASE JOIN THE COMMUNITY CONVERSATION ON WEDNESDAY, NOVEMBER 3rd for more information and next steps.

See the Woodstock PTA Newsletter of Oct. 31 st for links and more information.

Impact under Proposal A:

  1. Woodstock is now Creative Science and becomes a K-8 for now. PPS has said they will consider changing Creative Science to a K-5 soon.
  2. Woodstock Neighborhood school is closed. Neighborhood elementary K-5 students who remain in new Woodstock boundaries are given preferred admission to Creative Science as space allows. All students who reside outside of Woodstock’s new boundaries are re-assigned:
    • 32 Woodstock neighborhood students are re-assigned to Arleta Elementary
    • 21 Woodstock neighborhood students are re-assigned to Creston Elementary
    • 10 Woodstock neigbhorhood students are re-assigned to Woodmere Elementary
    • 27 Woodstock neighborhood students are re-assigned to Whitman Elementary
  3. Woodstock Neighborhood fifth graders go to middle schools including Lane (if moved to Woodmere or Whitman), or Kellogg (if moved to Arleta or Creston).
  4. Woodstock Mandarin Immersion Program moves to Bridger Elementary. Hosford Mandarin Immersion Program is transferred to Harrison Park Middle School. All Mandarin Immersion Program students in the district go to middle school at Harrison Park Middle School.

Impact under Proposal B:

  1. Woodstock becomes a stand alone Mandarin Immersion Program only. The Mandarin Immersion Program no longer gives preference for admission to those students residing within Woodstock boundaries but is open to all students across the district.
  2. Woodstock Neighborhood School closes. All current neighborhood students are re-assigned to various schools.
    • 41 Woodstock Neighborhood students are re-assigned to Lewis Elementary
    • 32 Neighborhood Woodstock students are re-assigned to Arleta Elementary
    • 21 Woodstock students are re-assigned to Creston Elementary
    • 69 Woodstock Neighborhood students are re-assigned to Woodmere Elementary
  3. Woodstock Neighborhood fifth graders are sent to middle schools based on new elementary school assignments. Creston will be re-assigned to Hosford Middle School. Lewis is assigned to Sellwood Middle School and Cleveland High School. Arleta is assigned to Kellogg Middle School and Franklin High School. Woodmere students are assigned to Lane Middle School.
  4. Woodstock Mandarin Immersion students are assigned to Lane Middle School. Harrison Park Mandarin Immersion students join them at Lane.

***High schools unknown but PPS has indicated Franklin is over capacity so it is likely Harrison Park will go to McDaniel (formerly Madison), Lane and Hosford will feed into Cleveland. Kellogg already feeds into Franklin***
